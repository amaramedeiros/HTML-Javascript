Interstellar

ABOUT CAST REVIEW

REVIEW

by Amara Medeiros 2/10/2022

Interstellar starts out with Cooper in his hometown. There have been terrible dust storms and the land had been very dry leading to all his crops burning. One night he found a drone in the sky and started chasing it in his truck while at the same time trying to gain control of it. He is successful, but it leads him to a classifiesd NASA base nearby. They weren't aware of who was at the gates, so he was taken in because people aren't supposed to know about this base. When they realize who they've stumbled upon they ask him to be the pilot for their mission.

It's explained to Cooper that the Earth is basically failing, and their mission is to find new life/habitable planet far out into space. Given that he now has 2 kids, his father to take care of, and him being retired; he's not interested. They show him around this new secret base and all the things they have planed, such as planting crops and etc. He leaves saying he will think it over. His daughter Murph doesen't want him to go, saying her books are falling off her bookshelf but she deciphers it as morse code for the word "STAY". Her dad thinks she's making everything up and reassures her he will come back and gives her his watch, and leaves for the mission.

A lot of events happen, he ends up losing contact from Earth and the crew realizes that they were really sent out to start new life on their own, and that there was no real plan to get back to Earth. Cooper and his crew are able to access another dimension, and this is where Cooper signals a message to his daughter Murph by using gravity to push her books off the shelf, trying to get him to stay on Earth and change the events that have already happened. Instead of traveling through the wormhole in space as intended, they use it's force to project the space craft back towards Earth. They return to Earth and his daughter, Murph, is old and dying (because time moves differently in other parts of space).
